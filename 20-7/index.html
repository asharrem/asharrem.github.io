New Zealand fiscal policy

The 20/7 series

A set of analytical notes on the proposed shift from direct taxation (income and company tax) toward a universal consumption base with a partial GST recovery rule—written for readers who follow public finance and political trade-offs closely.

← Previous Contents Next: Executive summary →

The common thread across these pages is the same design question: how revenue, equity, and administration change when the state stops taxing declared income and profit and instead captures flow through a 20% GST with a 65% recovery rate on business inputs—effectively a 7% operational wedge—while aiming for a stable fiscal surplus.

  6. Policy essay
